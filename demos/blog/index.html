Five Mornings in Porto

A slow-paced week along the Douro — coffee, custard tarts, and a soft kind of light that softens everything else.

We arrived on a Sunday, late, with the kind of luggage that fits in an overhead and a pocket of escudos left over from a previous trip. The taxi driver insisted we eat at the place his cousin owned. Of course we did.

Morning One — Ribeira

The waterfront wakes up slowly. Boats first, then bakeries, then everyone else. We followed the smell of warm bread to a corner counter and ordered two of everything.

Pastel de Nata — corner counter, Ribeira

Morning Two — Foz

A long walk west, against the river. The light here is different — colder, brighter, more honest. We bought espadrilles from a small shop and a wide canvas hat we did not need.

What we packed

We tried, this time, to bring less. Two notebooks, a film camera, a single pair of shoes each. We made a mental list at the end of every day.

Notebook, film, one extra shirt. That's all.

If you have a slow week and a passport, go.
